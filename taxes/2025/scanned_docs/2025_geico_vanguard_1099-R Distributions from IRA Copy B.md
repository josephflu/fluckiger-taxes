## Distribution Code Reference

**Code G — Direct Rollover**

A direct rollover of a distribution (other than a designated Roth account distribution) to a qualified plan, a section 403(b) plan, a governmental section 457(b) plan, or an IRA.

## Key Notes for Tax Filing

- **Form 1040 reporting:** Because the distribution code is **G (direct rollover)**, the $1,501.86 gross distribution is **not taxable** and is not subject to the 10% early withdrawal penalty. It must still be reported on Form 1040 (Line 5a for gross amount; Line 5b taxable amount = $0).
- **Taxable amount:** Box 2a is $0.00 and Box 2b "Taxable amount not determined" is checked — confirm with rollover destination account that the full amount was received. The taxable amount should remain $0 for a clean direct rollover.
- **No federal withholding:** Box 4 is $0.00. No estimated tax payment needed attributable to this distribution.
- **No state/local tax:** Box 14 and Box 17 are both $0.00. No state or local tax was withheld.
- **Form 5329:** Not required — Code G rollovers are not subject to the 10% additional tax on early distributions.
- **Verify rollover completion:** Confirm the receiving institution (rollover destination) reported receipt of the $1,501.86 to ensure the rollover was completed timely (generally within 60 days, though a direct rollover bypasses this requirement).
- **IRA/SEP/SIMPLE checkbox:** Not checked — this distribution originated from a 401(k) plan (GEICO 401(K) Savings Plan), not an IRA.
